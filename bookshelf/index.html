Bookshelf

Books I’ve read and recommend, organized in descending order by when I read them.

★★ Re-read or constantly reference
★ Highly recommended

The Origins of Efficiency (reading...)

Brian Potter

An exploration of how efficiency became central to modern industry and the historical development of industrial-scale problem solving.

A Brief History of Intelligence: Evolution, AI, and the Five Breakthroughs That Made Our Brains ★★

Max Bennett

A masterful synthesis weaving evolutionary biology, anthropology, and neuroscience into a compelling narrative of how human intelligence emerged.

Being Human

Lewis Dartnell

An engaging exploration of how human biology shaped major historical events, from disease destroying Scotland's Darien scheme to pathogens in warfare.

Quanta and Fields: The Biggest Ideas in the Universe ★★

Sean Carroll

Excellent overview of quantum mechanics and field theory.

The Biggest Ideas in the Universe: Space, Time, and Motion ★★

Sean Carroll

The best introduction to modern physics, perfectly balancing accessibility with mathematical depth.

Visual Differential Geometry and Forms: A Mathematical Drama in Five Acts ★★

Tristan Needham

A beautiful tour de force introduction to differential geometry with clear and engaging writing.

The Courage to Be Disliked: How to Free Yourself, Change Your Life and Achieve Real Happiness ★

Ichiro Kishimi & Fumitake Koga

A Plato Dialogue-based introduction to Adlerian psychology, challenging common assumptions about happiness and interpersonal relationships.

Endurance: Shackleton's Incredible Voyage

Alfred Lansing

A gripping masterclass in leadership chronicling Shackleton's 1914 Antarctic expedition and the crew's miraculous survival after their ship was crushed by ice.

Leadership in War: Essential Lessons from Those Who Made History

Andrew Roberts

I particularly enjoyed the chapter on Horatio Nelson.

Freedom's Forge: How American Business Produced Victory in World War II

Arthur Herman

Reveals how American industry transformed into an unprecedented wartime production machine that became the decisive force in WWII, with valuable lessons on management and scaling operations.

Skunk Works: A Personal Memoir of My Years at Lockheed

Ben R. Rich & Leo Janos

An insider's account of Lockheed's legendary aerospace division that showcases what's possible with ambitious engineering and the right organizational culture.

Good Strategy Bad Strategy: The Difference and Why It Matters ★

Richard Rumelt

One of the best business books, cutting through misconceptions to explain what strategy actually is and why most strategic plans fail.

Never Split the Difference: Negotiating As If Your Life Depended On It ★

Chris Voss

Practical negotiation techniques from FBI hostage negotiations that translate well to business contexts in sales and management.

Conflict: The Evolution of Warfare from 1945 to Ukraine

Andrew Roberts & David Petraeus

A comprehensive overview of major conflicts since WWII combined with General Petraeus's insider perspective on strategic decisions in Afghanistan and Iraq.

Nuclear War: A Scenario ★

Annie Jacobsen

A sobering minute-by-minute account of what nuclear war would actually look like, revealing that effective defense against ICBMs and SLBMs still doesn't exist.

How Not to Be Wrong: The Power of Mathematical Thinking

Jordan Ellenberg

Ellenberg's writing is clear and engaging, and he provides many examples of how mathematics can be used to solve real-world problems.

Shape: The Hidden Geometry of Information, Biology, Strategy, Democracy, and Everything Else

Jordan Ellenberg

Ellenberg provides a entertaining and informative overview of the hidden geometry of information, biology, strategy, democracy, and everything else.

The Man from the Future: The Visionary Life of John von Neumann

Ananyo Bhattacharya

The definitive biography of the 20th century's most brilliant polymath, whose contributions spanned mathematics, physics, computing, and game theory.

Elon Musk

Walter Isaacson

Isaacson's biography of Elon Musk, a good alternative to Vance's earlier book.

Elon Musk: Tesla, SpaceX, and the Quest for a Fantastic Future

Ashlee Vance

The definitive biography of Elon Musk, a must-read for anyone interested in his story.

The Innovator's Solution: Creating and Sustaining Successful Growth ★

Clayton M. Christensen

Christensen's follow-up to The Innovator's Dilemma provides actionable frameworks for creating and sustaining disruptive growth.

Chip War: The Fight for the World's Most Critical Technology

Chris Miller

A comprehensive history of the semiconductor industry that illuminates why semiconductors have become the most critical and contested technology of our era.

Proofs from THE BOOK ★★

Martin Aigner & Günter M. Ziegler

A collection of elegant mathematical proofs inspired by Paul Erdős's concept of 'The Book' where God keeps the most beautiful proof of each theorem.

When the Heavens Went on Sale: The Misfits and Geniuses Racing to Put Space Within Reach

Ashlee Vance

Chronicles the new space race driven by startups and entrepreneurs working to democratize access to space.

Crossing the Chasm: Marketing and Selling Disruptive Products to Mainstream Customers ★

Geoffrey A. Moore

Moore's classic explains why many promising products fail to reach mainstream markets and provides actionable frameworks for scaling from early adopters to the mainstream.

The Knowledge: How to Rebuild Civilization in the Aftermath of a Cataclysm ★★

Lewis Dartnell

A thought experiment guide to rebuilding civilization from scratch that reveals how dependent modern society is on accumulated knowledge and interdependence.

Origins: How the Earth Shaped Human History

Lewis Dartnell

Explores how geology, climate, and geography have fundamentally shaped human evolution and the development of civilizations.

Spacetime Physics ★★

Edwin F. Taylor & John Archibald Wheeler

A masterful introduction to special relativity by Wheeler.

How to Avoid a Climate Disaster: The Solutions We Have and the Breakthroughs We Need

Bill Gates

A pragmatic, numbers-driven approach to climate change.

The Field Guide to Global Payments

Sophia Goldberg

A comprehensive overview of how payment systems work globally, essential reading for anyone working in fintech.

Anatomy of the Swipe: Making Money Move

Ahmed Siddiqui

Demystifies the complex infrastructure behind credit card transactions and how money actually moves when you swipe.

Working Backwards: Insights, Stories, and Secrets from Inside Amazon ★

Colin Bryar & Bill Carr

Two former Amazon executives reveal the operational practices that enable Amazon's exceptional execution, from the six-page memo to the PR/FAQ document.

Think Like Amazon: 50 1/2 Ideas to Become a Digital Leader

John Rossman

Distills Amazon's cultural principles and practices that drive its success, though implementation requires genuine cultural change that many organizations resist.

Pragmatic Capitalism: What Every Investor Needs to Know About Money and Finance

Cullen Roche

A practical guide to understanding how the modern monetary system actually works, clearing up common misconceptions about banking and monetary policy.

The Good Parts of AWS: How to use what they offer without missing the good parts

Daniel Vassallo & Josh Pschorr

A curated guide to the most useful AWS services, cutting through the overwhelming array of options.

Effective Python: 90 Specific Ways to Write Better Python (2nd Edition)

Brett Slatkin

Slatkin's collection of Python best practices covers everything from fundamental idioms to advanced techniques.

What You Do Is Who You Are: How to Create Your Business Culture ★

Ben Horowitz

Draws leadership lessons from historical figures to show that culture is defined by actions, not values statements.

Anatomy of the Bear: Lessons from Wall Street's Four Great Bottoms

Russell Napier

Analyzes the four major bear markets in US history to identify patterns and lessons for investors, notably prescient in forecasting major market trends.

The Prize: The Epic Quest for Oil, Money & Power ★★

Daniel Yergin

Yergin's Pulitzer Prize-winning history of the oil industry reads like a thriller while explaining how petroleum shaped the 20th century.

Bad Blood: Secrets and Lies in a Silicon Valley Startup

John Carreyrou

The definitive account of Theranos's spectacular fraud, a cautionary tale about how charisma and prestigious backers can sustain vaporware for over a decade.

The Hard Thing About Hard Things: Building a Business When There Are No Easy Answers

Ben Horowitz

Brutally honest account of the challenges that don't make it into typical business books, from layoffs to managing your own psychology as CEO.

You Can Be a Stock Market Genius: Uncover the Secret Hiding Places of Stock Market Profits

Joel Greenblatt

A guide to finding value in special situations like spinoffs and mergers, with a valuable framework for thinking about complex situations.

Skin in the Game: Hidden Asymmetries in Daily Life

Nassim Nicholas Taleb

Argues that risk-sharing and accountability are essential for fairness and effective decision-making, exploring how asymmetries create systemic fragility.

The Everything Store: Jeff Bezos and the Age of Amazon

Brad Stone

The definitive account of Amazon's rise from online bookstore to global behemoth and how Bezos's 'regret minimization framework' shaped the company's expansion.

Designing Data-Intensive Applications: The Big Ideas Behind Reliable, Scalable, and Maintainable Systems

Martin Kleppmann

A comprehensive guide to the principles and trade-offs underlying modern data systems.

Creative Cash Flow Reporting: Uncovering Sustainable Financial Performance

Charles W. Mulford & Eugene E. Comiskey

Teaches how to analyze cash flow statements to uncover financial manipulation and see through how companies legally obscure their financial health.

Sustainable Energy – Without the Hot Air

David J.C. MacKay

A numbers-driven analysis that cuts through the rhetoric around renewable energy to show what's physically possible.

The Quest: Energy, Security, and the Remaking of the Modern World

Daniel Yergin

Yergin's sequel to The Prize examines the global energy landscape in the 21st century and how energy security continues to shape geopolitics.

The House of Morgan: An American Banking Dynasty and the Rise of Modern Finance

Ron Chernow

A sweeping narrative of how the Morgan banking empire shaped American and global finance across 150 years.

The Black Swan: The Impact of the Highly Improbable

Nassim Nicholas Taleb

Fooled by Randomness: The Hidden Role of Chance in Life and in the Markets

Nassim Nicholas Taleb

Titan: The Life of John D. Rockefeller, Sr.

Ron Chernow

A comprehensive biography that presents a nuanced portrait of America's first billionaire, revealing his systematic philanthropy alongside ruthless business practices.

Fifty Challenging Problems in Probability with Solutions ★★

Frederick Mosteller

A collection of challenging probability problems with solutions.

Problem Solving through Problems ★★

Loren C. Larson

A comprehensive guide to solving mathematical problems.
